03HAVE ANY QUESTIONS?

In my current role as a sales manager, I'm heavily involved in sales and data analysis, particularly in calculating growth rates, relative indicators, and more.

What I found most engaging in this job was creating algorithms, including those for identifying potential customers, building effective sales funnels, and monitoring recommended prices. Among these, I was particularly drawn to the algorithm for monitoring Recommended Retail Prices (RRP) due to its practicality.

This experience solidified my decision to transition to IT, where I can continuously learn, solve problems, and apply my algorithmic thinking skills for success.

Motivation is often described as a "carrot in front and carrot behind" scenario. In my case, I maintain a positive relationship with my previous job, and my transition to IT is primarily driven by the "carrot in front."

I left my previous job in October, having given a three-month notice. As a Team Lead in the wholesale industry, I was motivated by the company's ambitious goals, especially its expansion and the launch of a new brand. I extended my stay there for an additional month to assist with the new brand's market launch.

Nevertheless, my commitment to pursuing a career in IT remained unwavering. I recognized that the IT sector offers more opportunities for growth and development. Working in IT involves continuous learning, which aligns perfectly with my passion for logical thinking and problem-solving.

As I embark on this new journey, I'm well aware that one year is just the beginning. Nevertheless, I'm confident that this initial period will provide me with invaluable experience and the opportunity to enhance my skills. Collaborative teamwork and diverse tasks will be instrumental in my growth.

Simultaneously, I plan to engage in personal projects to broaden my horizons. I recognize that the English-speaking developer community is extensive, and to tap into this broader network, I'm actively working on improving my technical English skills. This involves regularly consuming educational content in English, such as watching instructional videos on YouTube.

Fearlessness in Decision-Making: I embrace the challenge of making bold decisions. For instance, my choice to transition to the IT field, even though it initially offers lower compensation and involves a steeper learning curve compared to my prior job, reflects my capacity for fearless decision-making. Throughout my academic journey, I consistently demonstrated this attribute, particularly when I assumed the role of a tech leader. When faced with tight project deadlines, I had to swiftly make critical decisions and shoulder the resulting responsibilities.

Dedication and Strategic Planning: Let me illustrate this trait with a case study: At the start of the year, I set a clear goal to elevate my spoken English proficiency to the B1-B2 level within the year. I meticulously crafted a detailed plan, focusing on grammar enhancement during the initial quarter. In the second quarter, recognizing the finite motivation of my peers, I proactively gathered 6-7 like-minded individuals, forming a dedicated speaking club. This strategic decision ensured consistent practice over the next six months. Over the past four months, I have consistently engaged in conversations with native English speakers, further demonstrating my dedication and effective planning.
